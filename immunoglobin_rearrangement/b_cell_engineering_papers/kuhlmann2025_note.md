# Heavy-chain immunoglobulin locus editing in rhesus macaque B cells to confer antibody production

Source and citation key: \cite{Kuhlmann2025IgHLocusEditingMacaqueBCells}

Why this source was read: candidate in the `b_cell_engineering_2026-07-16` rough-sources bucket; nonhuman-primate translational step beyond the mouse work in \cite{Nahmad2020EngineeredBCellsAntiHIV} / \cite{Huang2020VaccineElicitationEngineeredBCells}, promoted after full text became available.

## What was engineered?

Primary rhesus macaque B cells, edited at the IgH locus using CRISPR-Cas9 ribonucleoprotein electroporation, with an AAV6-delivered donor template inserting a VRC01 antibody cassette via homology-directed repair.

## Main engineering decision

Move the same locus-editing knock-in strategy validated in mice into a nonhuman primate model to test translational feasibility.

## Scale map

Intervention at the locus level. Consequences appear at the cellular level (fraction of cells actually expressing the antibody) and at the organism/health-status level (editing outcomes differed by prior infection status).

## Main measurement

DNA-level editing (indel formation) at the target locus was very efficient, but only a small fraction of edited cells actually displayed the antibody protein on their surface, and that fraction dropped further in animals with pre-existing viral infection.

## Dominant constraint

The gap between DNA-editing efficiency and actual functional protein expression — cutting the DNA is not the limiting step; getting a working, expressed protein out the other end is.

## Closest comparison

The mouse knock-in work in \cite{Nahmad2020EngineeredBCellsAntiHIV} and \cite{Huang2020VaccineElicitationEngineeredBCells} — one translational step earlier in the same overall strategy.

## One thing learned

A high editing-efficiency number at the DNA level can mask a much lower functional yield further downstream, and this gap can widen further in an already-perturbed immune system.

## Open questions

What causes the drop between successful gene insertion and surface protein expression, and would a similar gap appear when this strategy is eventually tried in humans?
