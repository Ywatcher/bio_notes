# Using the iCasp9 suicide strategy to control the growth and function of genome-edited B cells with redirected antigen specificity

Source and citation key: \cite{Leonard2025ICasp9GenomeEditedBCells}

Why this source was read: candidate in the `b_cell_engineering_2026-07-16` rough-sources bucket; the bucket's clearest example addressing the "what safety mechanisms are being explored" question, promoted after full text became available.

## What was engineered?

Primary B cells and B cell lines, edited at the IgH locus to insert, from a single integrated cassette, both a tumor-targeting single-chain antibody and an inducible caspase-9 "kill switch."

## Main engineering decision

Bundle a chemically triggerable safety off-switch together with the specificity-redirecting edit in one locus insertion, rather than treating safety as a separate, later add-on.

## Scale map

Intervention at the locus/molecular level. Consequences appear at the cellular level (rapid depletion on drug exposure) and at the organism level (tumor control in treated mice).

## Main measurement

Exposing edited cells to the small-molecule trigger killed the large majority of them within about three days in vitro, and tumor-bearing mice treated with the trigger survived meaningfully longer than untreated controls.

## Dominant constraint

Durability of the safety mechanism itself — over time, some edited cells became resistant to the kill switch by upregulating their own survival pathways, and the switch's own expression was downregulated in those escaping cells.

## Closest comparison

\cite{Boucher2025EngineeredHumanBCellsTumorAntigens} — both redirect BCR specificity toward tumor targets, but only this paper builds in an explicit safety switch.

## One thing learned

A safety switch engineered into the same construct as the therapeutic edit is not automatically permanent protection — drug resistance can still emerge over extended exposure.

## Open questions

Would a second, independent safety mechanism meaningfully reduce the resistance problem, or does stacking multiple switches just delay the same underlying failure mode?
