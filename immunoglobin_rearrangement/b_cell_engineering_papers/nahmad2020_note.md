# Engineered anti-HIV B cells enable memory retention, isotype switching, clonal expansion

Source and citation key: \cite{Nahmad2020EngineeredBCellsAntiHIV}

Why this source was read: candidate in the `b_cell_engineering_2026-07-16` rough-sources bucket; part of the batch comparison in `b_cell_engineering_comparison.md`, promoted to its own note after full text became available.

## What was engineered?

Primary mouse B cells, with the 3BNC117 anti-HIV broadly neutralizing antibody cassette inserted at the endogenous IgH locus via CRISPR/Cas9, delivered by recombinant AAV. The design lets the inserted antibody be expressed both as a membrane-bound receptor and, upon activation, as secreted antibody.

## Main engineering decision

Insert the antibody at the natural IgH locus rather than building a synthetic receptor construct from scratch, so the cell's own membrane/secreted expression switch stays intact and the engineered cells can still participate in normal B-cell biology.

## Scale map

Intervention is at the locus/molecular level (a stage-2, V(D)J-adjacent edit). The consequences of interest appear much further downstream, at the germinal-center and clonal-population level.

## Main measurement

After a boost immunization, donor-derived cells made up the large majority of antigen-binding germinal-center cells, and specific point mutations became enriched in individual animals — evidence that particular engineered clones were being favored by selection, not just persisting passively. Serum antibody titers rose substantially after the boost.

## Dominant constraint

Getting edited cells to actually compete successfully within germinal-center selection, not just express the inserted antibody.

## Closest comparison

\cite{Huang2020VaccineElicitationEngineeredBCells} — same overall goal (engineered BCR maturing through natural immune machinery), different design choices for the knock-in strategy.

## One thing learned

An engineered BCR is not fixed once inserted — it remains subject to the organism's own affinity-maturation and clonal-selection processes, which can further shape it after the edit.

## Open questions

Would the same degree of germinal-center dominance by engineered cells hold in a human immune system, where the competing endogenous repertoire is much broader than in these mouse models?
