# Engineered human B cells targeting tumor-associated antigens exhibit antigen presentation and antibody-mediated functions

Source and citation key: \cite{Boucher2025EngineeredHumanBCellsTumorAntigens}

Why this source was read: candidate in the `b_cell_engineering_2026-07-16` rough-sources bucket, promoted after full text became available.

## What was engineered?

Human B cells, edited with a CRISPR-based nuclease targeting the IgH J-C region, with an AAV6-delivered donor cassette encoding tumor-targeting antibody heavy and light chains.

## Main engineering decision

Redirect the natural B cell receptor toward a tumor antigen while preserving the cell's own antigen-processing and antibody-secretion machinery, so one engineered cell can both present antigen to T cells and secrete a functional antibody.

## Scale map

Intervention at the locus level. Consequences appear at the cellular/functional level (antigen presentation, antibody-mediated tumor-killing mechanisms) and at the population/safety level (the authors flag translocation risk from cutting the antibody locus).

## Main measurement

A large majority of edited cells expressed the intended receptor. Engineered cells triggered T-cell activation through antigen presentation and mediated multiple independent antibody-based tumor-killing mechanisms in functional assays.

## Dominant constraint

Safety — the authors specifically flag genotoxicity and IgH-locus translocation risk as an open, unresolved concern of this editing approach.

## Closest comparison

\cite{Leonard2025ICasp9GenomeEditedBCells} — same specificity-redirection idea, without a built-in safety switch.

## One thing learned

Engineering the BCR to fight tumors reuses the cell's entire existing toolkit (antigen presentation plus antibody secretion) rather than requiring a new mechanism — but that also means reusing the antibody locus's existing genomic instability risk.

## Open questions

Would combining this approach with a safety switch (as in \cite{Leonard2025ICasp9GenomeEditedBCells}) resolve the translocation-risk concern, or just relocate it?
