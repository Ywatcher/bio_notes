# Humanized mice enable in vivo evaluation of engineered plasma cell biology and therapeutic function

Source and citation key: \cite{Hill2026EngineeredPlasmaCellBiology}

Why this source was read: candidate in the `b_cell_engineering_2026-07-16` rough-sources bucket, includes a BCR surface-display screening step; promoted after full text became available.

## What was engineered?

Human B cells edited via CRISPR-based homology-directed repair to carry therapeutic antibody genes, then differentiated into engineered plasma cells and evaluated in a humanized mouse model.

## Main engineering decision

Build a screening method — inserted at the antibody heavy-chain constant region — that ranks candidate antibody sequences by how well they get secreted, before committing to one sequence for the full therapeutic construct.

## Scale map

Intervention partly at the locus level (the screening construct) and partly at the cellular-engineering level (differentiation into plasma cells). Consequences appear at the organism level: persistence and function in a living animal over months.

## Main measurement

Engineered plasma cells persisted and continued secreting antibody for more than three months in the humanized mouse model; a strongly-secreting candidate sequence reached close to its predicted antibody levels and neutralized a test virus effectively, while a poorly-secreting candidate underperformed sharply.

## Dominant constraint

Not every antibody sequence secretes well from an engineered plasma cell — sequence-dependent secretion differences are large and need to be screened for rather than assumed from binding properties alone.

## Closest comparison

\cite{Liu2025EngineeredBCellFactorIX} — same broad idea of plasma cells as a secretion platform, but this paper keeps a foothold at the antibody locus for its screening step.

## One thing learned

Before committing to one antibody design for a plasma-cell therapy, screening multiple sequences for secretion behavior can matter as much as the antibody's binding properties.

## Open questions

Could this surface-display screening approach be combined with locus-editing strategies like \cite{Boucher2025EngineeredHumanBCellsTumorAntigens} or \cite{Leonard2025ICasp9GenomeEditedBCells} to pre-select variants likely to express well before building the full therapeutic construct?
